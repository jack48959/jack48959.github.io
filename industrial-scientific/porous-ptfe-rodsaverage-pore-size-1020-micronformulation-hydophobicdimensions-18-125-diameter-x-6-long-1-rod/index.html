Temperature Resistant From -328° F (-200° C) to +500° F (260° C) Non-Reactive, High Molecular Weight Fluoropolymer Compound Manufactured in the United States

PTFE Porous Rods, Tubes & Sheets are excellent for use in extreme heat up to +500° F (260° C) and resistant to a low-end temperature of -328° (-200° C). PTFE is used in applications where other plastics break down due to their inability to be resistant to chemicals and heat. It is said that PTFE exhibits a combination of mechanical, electrical, chemical temperature and friction resistant properties unmatched by any other substrate. It is a non-reactive, high molecular weight fluoropolymer compound used in aerospace, pharmaceutical, chemical and petrochemical fields. Where other porous media fail in acid, alkalis and solvent environments, PTFE provides the ability to work in the extremes. This PTFE fluoropolymer is ideal for high temp venting, chemical diffusion and a host of filtration application for solvents, gases and medications. After using, it can be cleaned and sterilized by autoclave or ETO. DO NOT USE PTFE in applications where you have molten alkali metal, fluorine or strong fluorination agents.
