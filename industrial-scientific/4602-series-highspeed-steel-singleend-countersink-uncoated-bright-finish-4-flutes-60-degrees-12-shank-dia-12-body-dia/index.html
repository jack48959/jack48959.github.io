The Union Butterfield 4602 series high-speed steel single-end countersink with an uncoated (bright) finish creates a 60-degree conical opening that can be used for holding material between machine centers. The four flutes clear chips away to prevent clogging and have more wear resistance than three flutes. High-speed steel (HSS) provides hardness for wear resistance when cutting ferrous and nonferrous materials. The uncoated finish has no additional surface treatment or coating. The tool has a single cutting end and a round shank that accommodates most drill chucks. Countersinks are fluted cutting tools that cut a conical or gradually widened opening for a hole into the surface of a workpiece, which provides a seat for a screw or rivet head. They are also used to create center holes for a machine center, deburr surfaces, and enlarging existing holes. The countersink’s body diameter should be the same size or larger than what is being countersunk.
