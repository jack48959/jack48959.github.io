Cabinet Installation System (HIYW188)

Professional results with DIY skills - The system is designed for one person cabinet installation in 3 basic steps: 1)Attach the rail to the wall. 2) Mount the brackets on the cabinets. 3) Hang the cabinets on the rail.

  1. Attach the rail- the wall rails are pre-punched with holes 3/4" on center to enable at least one fastener to be attached to every wall stud. The rail is easily leveled, shimmed, aligned and secured.
  2. Mount the brackets- the auto aligning brackets allow each cabinet to be supported independently on the rail without any localized stress on it or an adjacent cabinet.
  3. Hang the cabinets- with the cabinets fully suspended on the rail via the brackets, they can be moved, aligned and the faces attached to one another. The top of the rail sits 1 and 3/16" above the top of the cabinet and is typically hidden by crown moulding or other readily available trim. The hanging system typically holds a cabinet about 5/16" off the wall which is normally covered by scribe trim. For installation on other than wood framing such as metal stud, brick, block or concrete walls, we recommend using 3/16" Toggler brand Snap-Toggles through the metal studs and 3/16" Hex head Tapcon fasteners for brick, block and concrete. Presently, the hardware kits only contain fasteners for wood framing installation.

Hang-It-Yourself is a universal fit, auto aligning, DIY wall cabinet hanging system. It works on any pre-assembled or ready to assemble (RTA), framed or frameless wall cabinet that typically would be attached to a wall with screws through the cabinet rear panel. Each installation kit includes all the “rail length” specific hardware needed to install the rails and mount the cabinets on wood framed walls. Only the front face attachment screws are not included due to the varying sizes and styles recommended by each cabinet manufacturer. Figuring What You Need- 1) Calculate the overall length, in inches, of the cabinets that are to be installed in each layout space. 2) Select the rail kit(s) that provide this length in each layout space. It is acceptable for the rail length to be up to 8 inches shorter than the cabinet layout space. The 48" and 94" rails that make up the 94", 142" and 188" kits can easily be trimmed with a hacksaw. The kits ship in sturdy cardboard tubes.
